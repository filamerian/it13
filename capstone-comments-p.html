Statement of the Problem (Revised)
Despite growing demand for operational efficiency in fitness centers, Urban Fitness Gym Roxas City branch continues to utilize manual systems for member management, subscription validation, payment processing, and product sales. This legacy approach presents several critical challenges:

  1. The current check-in and attendance system is inefficient, often resulting in long queues and delayed verification during peak hours, which negatively affects the member experience.
  2. The lack of a secure, automated access control system introduces risks of unauthorized entry and inconsistent enforcement of subscription-based privileges, compromising both operational integrity and member safety.
  3. Manual tracking of payments and subscription renewals leads to delays, errors, and incomplete records. This creates administrative burdens and undermines financial accuracy and customer trust.
  4. The absence of a structured inventory system for gym products - such as apparel, supplements, and accessories - results in stock inconsistencies, missed sales opportunities, and poor resource management.
  5. Without an integrated data management system, the gym lacks real-time insights into member attendance, facility usage, and purchasing behavior, restricting its ability to make informed decisions on scheduling, marketing, and service enhancements.

General Objective (Revised)
To design and implement an RFID-based management system that enhances operational efficiency, security, and customer experience at Urban Fitness Gym Roxas City branch by automating member tracking, subscription validation, payment processing, and product inventory management.

Specific Objectives (Revised)

  1. To develop an RFID-based check-in and attendance logging system that streamlines member verification during peak hours, reduces queue times, and enhances the overall customer experience.
  2. To implement a secure access control mechanism integrated with membership tiers, ensuring accurate validation of active subscriptions and preventing unauthorized entry into gym facilities.
  3. To design a cashless payment and subscription renewal module that automates transaction recording, minimizes errors, and supports accurate financial tracking for both members and administrators.
  4. To build an inventory management system for in-gym products, enabling real-time stock updates, item categorization, and alerts for low-supply items to support efficient sales and restocking decisions.
  5. To incorporate a centralized data analytics dashboard that visualizes member attendance trends, facility usage metrics, and purchasing behavior to inform strategic decisions on scheduling, marketing campaigns, and service improvements.

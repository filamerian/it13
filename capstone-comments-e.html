INTERACTIVE GAME FOR LEARNING CAPIZNON LANGUAGE AND CULTURE

CHAPTER I – INTRODUCTION

I. Overview of the Current State of Technology
Strengths:

  • Establishes the relevance of digital tools - particularly games - for modern education and youth engagement.
  • Identifies an important cultural gap: the underrepresentation of regional languages like Capiznon in interactive learning platforms.
  • Grounds the project in meaningful social impact: language preservation and cultural continuity.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Include national and global statistics (e.g., UNESCO or Ethnologue data) on language endangerment to reinforce urgency.
  • Avoid generalizations such as “there remains a significant gap” - instead, specify what existing platforms lack.
  • Expand discussion of Capiznon's linguistic status with comparison to Hiligaynon and Kinaray-a to clarify its uniqueness.

Recommended Enhancements:
Incorporate references to previous digital preservation efforts for Philippine languages (e.g., Blaan, Maranao) to establish conceptual precedent.

II. Desired State of Technology
Strengths:

  • Clearly defines the game’s dual purpose: language learning and cultural immersion.
  • Emphasizes a shift from rote learning to meaningful, contextual interaction.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Describe what “immersive” means in terms of gameplay (e.g., avatar-based storytelling, interactive folklore quests).
  • Clarify whether the game is mobile-first, web-based, or cross-platform.
  • Define the target demographic more precisely (e.g., Grade 6-12 students, young adults, diaspora communities).

Recommended Enhancements:
Provide a visual mockup or gameplay flow that shows how learners progress through both language and culture layers.

III. Statement of the Problem
Strengths:

  • Identifies the waning use of Capiznon among youth and the lack of engaging tools to address this.
  • Connects the problem with the broader context of cultural erosion.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Frame the statement as formal research questions:
    • How can game-based learning enhance retention of Capiznon vocabulary and syntax?
    • What cultural content formats (e.g., folklore, festivals) improve learner engagement?

Recommended Enhancements:
Introduce a short contextual paragraph linking this linguistic challenge to globalization and media dominance of Tagalog/English.

IV. Objectives of the Study
Strengths:

  • The general objective is well-defined, emphasizing both linguistic and cultural goals.
  • Specific objectives are purposeful and aligned with system design components.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Reword unclear phrases like “create an element of Capiznon culture…” to “embed cultural content through narrative and gameplay.”
  • Add performance indicators to each objective (e.g., vocabulary retention scores, cultural understanding feedback, engagement metrics).

Recommended Enhancements:
Organize objectives thematically: Language Learning, Cultural Enrichment, Accessibility, Pedagogical Effectiveness.

V. Theoretical Framework
Strengths:

  • Anchors the study in Constructivist Learning Theory, which is appropriate for game-based and experiential learning.
  • Highlights active learner participation and real-world application.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Complement Constructivism with the Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning (Mayer) for interface design justification.
  • Include sources or theorists who support game-based language acquisition.

Recommended Enhancements:
Discuss how gameplay mechanics (e.g., problem-solving, decision-making) activate the learner’s construction of meaning and cultural relevance.

VI. Conceptual Framework
Strengths:

  • Inputs, Processes, and Outputs are logically defined and directly linked to game structure and learning outcomes.
  • Includes key components such as engagement, assessment, interaction, and feedback.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Expand on “Participants”: who are they demographically and linguistically?
  • Elaborate on how each Process variable contributes to the desired Output (e.g., how “learning progression” leads to “language proficiency”).

Recommended Enhancements:
Provide a system diagram (e.g., annotated Figure 1.1) that shows flow from onboarding to content mastery and cultural immersion.

VII. Operational Definition of Terms
Strengths:

  • Clearly explains foundational terms relevant to the project.
  • Makes the language accessible to technical and non-technical audiences.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Include academic citations for terms like “interactive game,” “cultural awareness,” and “language learning.”
  • Define additional terms used later in methodology or review (e.g., “adaptive gameplay,” “mini-games,” “storytelling immersion”).

Recommended Enhancements:
Format this section as a glossary with source attribution (APA/IEEE format).

VIII. Scope and Limitations
Strengths:

  • Scope addresses both linguistic and cultural domains, with flexibility for dialectal variations.
  • Limitations acknowledge surface-level cultural representation and learning depth challenges.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Present content in structured format:
    • Scope: Language taught (Capiznon), cultural focus, gameplay format.
    • Limitations: Cultural oversimplification risks, advanced fluency gaps, idiom coverage.
  • Address hardware/software constraints (e.g., Android compatibility, offline access).

Recommended Enhancements:
Include geographic scope (e.g., target schools or communities), time frame for testing, and user role types.

IX. Significance of the Study
Strengths:

  • Clearly articulates the project’s contribution to language preservation, education, and cultural identity.
  • Identifies key stakeholders: learners, educators, researchers, developers.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Strengthen discussion of interdisciplinary value - for example, how sociolinguistics, game design, and pedagogy intersect.
  • Avoid overly casual phrases - revise “fun and interactive game” to “engaging game-based platform.”

Recommended Enhancements:
Add linkage to UNESCO’s cultural preservation goals or DepEd’s mother tongue-based multilingual education (MTB-MLE) initiatives.

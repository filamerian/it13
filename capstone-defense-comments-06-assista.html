Summary

ASSISTA addresses an important local problem: slow, paper-based processing of AICS applications that creates delays, fragmented records, and poor communication with beneficiaries. The proposal is relevant and well-structured. Strengths include a clear problem overview, a practical set of modules (registration, tracking, notification, reporting, awareness), and the use of Agile (Scrum). Areas to improve are clearer objectives (make them outcome-focused), more detail in methodology (sprint plan, sample sizes), technical design details (ERD, security, backup), and a stronger evaluation plan tied to standards and targets.

Chapter III - Methodology

Your methodology chapter is well-written and clearly explains why Agile (Scrum) was chosen. The structure flows logically from requirement analysis to deployment and maintenance, and you identified the tools and technologies correctly. It shows that you understand the process of developing a system.

However, several parts need improvement to make the methodology stronger and more consistent:

  1. Agile (Scrum) Details: You explained Scrum well, but you did not specify sprint length (for example, 2 weeks) and expected deliverables per sprint. This detail will make your plan more realistic and easier to follow.

  2. Design Phase: You mentioned Figma, UML diagrams, and database schema, but the ERD and schema are not shown in this section. Add at least one ERD and a sample database design to prove the data structure supports your objectives.

  3. Development Phase: You listed the technologies (React, Laravel, MySQL), which is good. To improve, briefly explain why these tools were chosen (for example, Laravel for security, React for modular UI, MySQL for scalability). This shows you did not just pick tools randomly.

  4. Testing and Verification: The testing part is strong because you included UAT with CSWDO staff. Still, it will be better if you add clear acceptance targets (for example, "System should achieve 95 percent accuracy in test cases" or "average usability rating of 4 out of 5").

  5. Deployment Phase: You explained local deployment well. Strengthen this by adding your plan for data security and backups during deployment.

  6. Maintenance and Evolution: This section is good because you listed future upgrades. To improve, clarify which upgrades can be done within the capstone timeline and which ones are only recommendations for future development.

  7. Scrum Framework Overview: This is detailed, but avoid repeating what was already said under Agile Methodology. Make this shorter by focusing on how your team used each part (Product Backlog, Sprint Planning, etc.), not just the definition.

  8. Tools and Technologies: You listed tools clearly. Improve this by creating a simple table that matches each tool with its purpose (for example, “React.js – frontend development”). This makes it easier for readers and panelists to understand.

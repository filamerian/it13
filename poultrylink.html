Capstone Problem Analysis

PoultryLink: A Web-Based Multi-Vendor Marketplace for Capiz

Overall Evaluation

This proposal has a strong problem domain and high social relevance, but in its current form it is one of the most over-scoped projects among the submissions reviewed.

The group is attempting to build:

  • an e-commerce marketplace
  • a permit verification system
  • a biosecurity tracking platform
  • a poultry inventory management system
  • a traceability system
  • a logistics monitoring system

These are essentially multiple capstone projects combined into one.

The proposal has potential, but it requires significant scope reduction before it becomes a realistic BSIT capstone.

Strengths

1. Real Industry Problem

The poultry industry faces genuine challenges:

  • paper-based records
  • traceability difficulties
  • pricing disputes
  • supply chain transparency issues

These are legitimate problems.

2. Localized Application

Unlike generic marketplace systems, this proposal focuses on:

  • poultry sellers in Capiz
  • small-scale resellers
  • local consumers

This improves relevance and practicality.

3. Clear Stakeholders

The beneficiaries are well-defined:

  • poultry farm operators
  • resellers
  • consumers
  • administrators

This makes system evaluation easier.

4. Good Potential for Community Impact

A successful implementation could improve:

  • transaction transparency
  • product traceability
  • record management
  • local poultry trade efficiency

Major Concerns

1. The Project is Trying to Solve Too Many Problems

Current problems include:

Trading

  • buyer-seller transactions

Pricing

  • catch-weight computation

Compliance

  • permit verification

Biosecurity

  • disease traceability

Inventory

  • hatch and lay monitoring

Logistics

  • route tracking

Each of these could be an independent capstone project.

2. Unrealistic Government Verification Claims

The proposal includes:

Real-time BAI/NMIS Permit Verification

This is a major red flag.

Questions a panel will ask:

  • Does BAI provide an API?
  • Does NMIS provide an API?
  • Have you secured access?

Most likely the answer is no.

Recommendation:

Replace:
Real-time permit verification
with
Permit upload and administrator validation

3. Marketplace Alone is Already a Large Project

A multi-vendor marketplace requires:

  • vendor management
  • product management
  • order processing
  • transaction handling
  • buyer management
  • reporting

This already represents a substantial capstone scope.

Adding biosecurity, logistics, and inventory tracking creates unnecessary complexity.

4. Biological Inventory Module is Difficult to Justify

Hatch and lay date tracking

Questions:

  • Why is this necessary for a marketplace?
  • Is PoultryLink a trading platform or a farm management system?

This feature creates confusion regarding the system's purpose.

5. Source-to-Destination Route Navigation is Unnecessary

This feature appears disconnected from the core problem.

The project is not a logistics platform.

This should be removed unless the group can clearly justify its necessity.

Problem Statement Assessment

The current problem statement is actually describing multiple separate problems.

Core Problem:

Poultry trading in Capiz is largely conducted through manual processes that make transactions, record management, and product traceability inefficient.

Recommended Problem Statements

  1. Poultry sellers and resellers in Capiz rely on manual methods for managing transactions and product listings.
  2. Buyers have limited information regarding the source and traceability of poultry products.
  3. Product and permit records are commonly stored in paper-based formats, making verification and monitoring difficult.
  4. Small-scale poultry sellers have limited access to digital platforms for marketing and selling their products.
  5. There is no centralized system that integrates poultry trading, product traceability, and seller information in a single platform.

Recommended Objectives

General Objective

To design and develop a web-based poultry trading and traceability platform for poultry sellers, resellers, and consumers in Capiz.

Specific Objectives

  1. To develop a multi-vendor marketplace for poultry sellers and resellers.
  2. To provide a centralized platform for managing poultry products and transactions.
  3. To implement product traceability through seller and source information records.
  4. To allow sellers to upload and manage compliance-related documents and permits.
  5. To generate transaction and monitoring reports for administrators.

Features to Keep

  • User registration
  • Vendor management
  • Product listings
  • Order management
  • Product source information
  • Permit upload
  • Transaction records
  • Reporting

Features Recommended for Removal

  • Real-time BAI/NMIS verification
  • Source-to-destination navigation
  • Biological inventory tracking
  • Complex compliance automation

Novelty Assessment

Current Novelty Claim

Weak — Marketplace systems already exist.

Stronger Novelty Position

A localized poultry trading marketplace integrated with product traceability and permit management for small-scale poultry sellers in Capiz.

Progress Assessment

Current Status

Working:

  • Login page

Not Started:

  • Most major modules

This is concerning because the most complex features remain unfinished.

The group should begin developing the marketplace core immediately rather than spending time on advanced compliance and biosecurity modules.

Coordinator Verdict

Status: Needs Major Revision


Reasons

  • Scope is excessively large.
  • Multiple unrelated systems are combined into one project.
  • Government permit verification claims are unrealistic.
  • System identity is unclear.
  • Most major features have not yet started.

Recommendation

Reduce the project to a Poultry Trading and Traceability Marketplace. Focus on transactions, seller management, product traceability, and permit uploads. Remove advanced government integration, logistics navigation, and biological inventory management.

With these revisions, the project can become a strong, realistic, and defendable BSIT capstone.

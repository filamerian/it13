Proposed System Analysis

Threads of the Forgotten Chant

Overall Evaluation

This is one of the most creative and culturally significant proposals submitted. Unlike typical inventory or monitoring systems, it focuses on the preservation and promotion of local cultural heritage through interactive technology.

However, from a BSIT capstone perspective, the project currently suffers from a major issue:

The scope resembles a commercial educational game rather than a manageable BSIT capstone project.

The group itself already recognized this by indicating that scope reduction is necessary.

Strengths

1. Strong Cultural Relevance

The project addresses:

  • Suguidanon epic chanting
  • Panubok embroidery
  • Oral storytelling
  • Panay-Bukidnon traditions

These are legitimate cultural heritage topics that are often underrepresented in digital learning tools.

This gives the project strong community and educational value.

2. Clear Target Users

The users are clearly identified:

  • FCU Elementary Students
  • Teachers
  • FCU Elementary Department

This allows for realistic testing and evaluation.

3. Strong Localization

Many educational games are generic.

This project is unique because it focuses on:

  • Tapaz, Capiz
  • Panay-Bukidnon culture
  • Local heritage preservation

This localization is the strongest novelty of the proposal.

4. Educational Purpose

The system aims to:

  • increase cultural awareness
  • improve engagement
  • support heritage education

This gives the project a meaningful purpose beyond entertainment.

Major Concerns

1. Problem Validation is Weak

The proposal assumes:

Students have limited awareness of local cultural heritage.

The panel will likely ask:

  • How was this measured?
  • Was a survey conducted?
  • Were teachers interviewed?
  • Are there pre-test results showing low cultural knowledge?

Currently, the problem appears assumed rather than validated.

2. Scope is Extremely Large

Current features include:

  • split-screen co-op gameplay
  • explorer role
  • guide role
  • combat mechanics
  • puzzle solving
  • chant restoration
  • artifact discovery
  • AI-assisted hints
  • digital museum
  • database integration

This is far beyond a normal BSIT capstone.

In reality, this is closer to an indie game development project.

3. Combat Mechanics are Difficult to Justify

One of the largest concerns is:

combat mechanics

Panel question:

"How does combat contribute to cultural heritage learning?"

The connection is weak.

Combat appears to be a game feature rather than a learning feature.

Recommendation: Remove combat entirely.

4. AI-Assisted Learning is Poorly Defined

The proposal mentions:

AI-assisted hints

Questions that may arise:

  • What AI model will be used?
  • How will it be trained?
  • What data will support it?
  • Is internet access required?

If these questions cannot be answered clearly, the AI feature becomes a liability.

Recommendation: Replace AI with:
  • rule-based hints
  • scripted guidance
  • adaptive hint progression

5. Split-Screen Co-op Adds Significant Complexity

The project includes:

  • two player roles
  • simultaneous interaction
  • shared puzzle solving

This greatly increases development difficulty.

For a capstone prototype, single-player gameplay may be sufficient.

Problem Statement Assessment

The actual problem is not:

"Culture is being forgotten."

The stronger problem is:

Elementary students have limited engagement with localized cultural heritage due to the lack of interactive digital learning tools.

This is measurable and defendable.

Recommended Problem Statements

  1. Elementary students have limited exposure to localized cultural heritage topics such as Suguidanon chanting, Panubok embroidery, and Panay-Bukidnon traditions.
  2. Existing cultural learning materials are primarily lecture-based and lack interactive digital components.
  3. Students are generally more engaged with digital games and multimedia than traditional instructional methods.
  4. There is currently no localized educational game that promotes the cultural heritage of Tapaz, Capiz within the FCU Elementary Department.
  5. Teachers have limited access to interactive digital tools for teaching local cultural heritage.

Recommended Objectives

General Objective

To design and develop an interactive educational game that promotes awareness and understanding of the cultural heritage of Tapaz, Capiz among FCU Elementary students.

Specific Objectives

  1. To develop a game-based learning environment featuring localized cultural heritage content.
  2. To implement interactive puzzles based on Panubok embroidery and Suguidanon traditions.
  3. To provide cultural information through exploration and storytelling mechanics.
  4. To incorporate guided assistance that helps students progress through educational activities.
  5. To evaluate the effectiveness of the game in improving student engagement and cultural awareness.

Recommended Scope Reduction

Keep

  • One playable level
  • Cultural storytelling
  • Panubok-inspired puzzles
  • Chant restoration activity
  • Cultural information system
  • Simple hint mechanism

These alone are enough for a capstone.

Remove

  • Combat system
  • Multiple game worlds
  • Large-scale artifact collection
  • Advanced AI implementation
  • Extensive co-op mechanics

These features dramatically increase risk without significantly improving educational outcomes.

Novelty Assessment

Originality: 9/10

Technical Complexity: 8/10

Practical Value: 8/10

Feasibility (Current Scope): 4/10

Feasibility (Reduced Scope): 8/10

The novelty comes from:

  • local cultural heritage integration
  • educational gamification
  • Panay-Bukidnon content

Not from AI, combat, or co-op gameplay.

Progress Assessment

Current Status

Completed:

  • concept
  • planning
  • narrative direction

Not Started:

  • actual game development
  • gameplay mechanics
  • puzzles
  • database
  • testing

This is concerning because June 30, 2026 is very close and there is currently no playable prototype.

Coordinator Verdict

Status: Approved with Major Scope Reduction


Reasons

  • Strong educational and cultural value.
  • Highly localized and unique.
  • Appropriate target users.
  • Good potential for community impact.

Required Revisions

  • Validate the problem through surveys or pre-tests.
  • Remove combat mechanics.
  • Simplify or remove AI components.
  • Reduce co-op complexity.
  • Focus on one complete playable educational experience rather than multiple game systems.

If the group follows its own realization and limits the project to a single cultural learning environment with puzzles and storytelling, this can become one of the most unique and successful BSIT capstone projects in the batch.


However, if they attempt to build everything currently proposed, the likelihood of completing a working system on time is very low.

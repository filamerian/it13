Statement of the Problem
Lawaan Integrated School currently faces persistent challenges in managing and maintaining its property inventory due to reliance on a manual system. These limitations negatively impact operational efficiency, transparency, and timely access to instructional resources. The specific problems identified are as follows:

  1. Inaccurate and Delayed Asset Reporting
    The manual system lacks real-time updates, resulting in outdated or inconsistent inventory records that hinder accurate asset monitoring.
  2. Difficulty in Locating and Tracking Equipment
    There is no centralized method for identifying the current status or location of physical items such as projectors, computers, or furniture, leading to confusion and inefficiencies.
  3. Frequent Discrepancies During Audits
    Inventory audits are time-consuming and prone to human error, resulting in mismatches between physical assets and recorded data.
  4. Limited Accountability and Transparency
    Without automated logs and role-based tracking, it becomes difficult to assign responsibility for asset use, movement, or loss.
  5. Delayed Request and Approval for New Resources
    Teachers and staff face prolonged wait times when requesting equipment due to manual filing, lack of notification systems, and poor documentation of request histories.

General Objective
This capstone project aims to develop and implement a responsive, QR code-enabled Property Inventory System that enhances asset tracking, improves audit accuracy, and streamlines equipment request workflows for Lawaan Integrated School, thereby promoting operational transparency and resource accountability.
Specific Objectives

  1. Automate Inventory Management Tasks
    Design a digital system that replaces manual asset logging with QR code scanning, enabling real-time inventory updates and minimizing human error.
  2. Enhance Asset Visibility and Location Tracking
    Provide an interface that allows users to efficiently locate school properties, view item details, and monitor status or movement across departments.
  3. Reduce Audit Discrepancies and Inspection Time
    Integrate features that support swift and accurate inventory audits by maintaining synchronized records and automated logs.
  4. Establish Role-Based Access and Approval Workflow
    Implement user access levels (e.g., admin, teacher) with permission controls for requesting, approving, and managing school assets.
  5. Generate Reliable Reports for Planning and Accountability
    Enable report generation on equipment availability, usage patterns, and asset lifecycle data for administrative decision-making and transparency.

Recommendations / Revised Statement of the Problem:

Bullying continues to be a pervasive issue in educational institutions, manifesting in both physical and digital environments. At Filamer Christian University (FCU) High School, recent trends have shown fluctuations in reported cases, with a noted decline following manual interventions by the Guidance Office. However, this decrease does not necessarily reflect full resolution—many cases remain unreported due to fear of retaliation, stigma, and a lack of safe, accessible mechanisms for disclosure.
The current reporting protocol at FCU heavily relies on manual documentation, which presents several limitations in terms of confidentiality, responsiveness, and data handling. Students are often required to verbally report or fill out written forms, processes that inherently compromise anonymity and deter victims or witnesses from seeking help. Furthermore, the system lacks real-time analytics and integration with counselor workflows, making early interventions slow and data-driven decision-making nearly impossible.
To ensure a safer, student-centered reporting environment, the following key issues must be addressed:

  1. Absence of an Anonymous Digital Channel: The lack of a secure and anonymized reporting platform discourages students from disclosing incidents of bullying, particularly those that are emotionally or socially sensitive.
  2. Limited Emotional Support and Follow-Up Mechanisms: Without dedicated support pathways following a report, victims may experience prolonged psychological distress or uncertainty about case outcomes.
  3. Inefficiency in Addressing Cyberbullying Cases: The manual system is not equipped to respond to online bullying, leaving digital harassment underreported and unresolved.
  4. Fear of Social Judgment and Reprisal: Students often refrain from reporting due to concerns about peer labeling, administrative bias, or future targeting by aggressors.
  5. Lack of Integrated Response Framework: The absence of a centralized digital system inhibits data consolidation, timely notifications, and pattern recognition needed for effective preventive action.

The need for an anonymous, secure, and responsive reporting tool is therefore critical—not only for capturing accurate data on bullying incidents, but also for fostering a psychologically safe campus culture. E-GUIDANCE CONNECT seeks to bridge this institutional gap by offering an integrated system that empowers students to speak up while enabling school counselors and administrators to act swiftly and empathetically.

Recommendations:

General Objective
To design, develop, and deploy a secure, anonymous digital reporting platform that empowers students at Filamer Christian University to disclose bullying incidents without fear, while enabling counselors and administrators to act promptly and effectively through integrated data management, emotional support mechanisms, and responsive intervention workflows.

Specific Objectives

  1. To implement a secure, anonymous digital reporting system that allows students to disclose bullying incidents without revealing their identity, directly addressing the absence of an anonymous digital channel.
  2. To integrate emotional support resources within the platform, such as counselor access, response prompts, and guided mental health content, in response to the limited support and follow-up mechanisms for victims.
  3. To design reporting forms and backend processes that accommodate online harassment through features like category tagging, timestamp logging, and secure digital evidence submission—answering the inefficiency in addressing cyberbullying cases.
  4. To establish user-centered interface design and anonymity safeguards that reduce fear of exposure and judgment, directly combating the fear of social reprisal and stigma associated with manual reporting.
  5. To build a centralized system that enables real-time notification, counselor routing, and report monitoring, thereby resolving the lack of an integrated response framework and improving intervention speed and accuracy.

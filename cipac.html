Proposed System Analysis

CIPAC: An Application for Capiz Indigenous Peoples' Arts and Crafts

Overall Evaluation

This proposal has a meaningful cultural and community-centered objective because it focuses on promoting and preserving indigenous arts and crafts in Capiz. The project also addresses digital visibility and market accessibility for indigenous artisans.

However, in its current form, the proposal is still too broad and lacks clear problem validation. The system currently resembles a full-scale e-commerce marketplace platform, which may become difficult to complete within the BSIT capstone timeline.

The project has strong potential if the scope is significantly reduced and the focus is narrowed to manageable core features.

Strengths

1. Strong Cultural and Community Relevance

The project supports:

  • indigenous artisans
  • cultural preservation
  • local craftsmanship promotion
  • community livelihood opportunities

This gives the proposal strong social value.

2. Real Digital Visibility Problem

The proposal correctly identifies that many indigenous artisans have limited opportunities to market their products beyond their local communities.

An indigenous artisan in Capiz cannot sell their handmade crafts to customers outside their local community because there is no dedicated online platform to showcase and promote their products.

This is a legitimate digital accessibility and marketing problem.

3. Appropriate BSIT Components

The proposed system includes:

  • user account management
  • artisan profiles
  • e-commerce functionality
  • administrative controls

These align with BSIT competencies involving databases, web systems, and information management.

Major Concerns

1. Problem Validation is Weak

The proposal states:

"Not sure"

regarding whether the problem is validated or observed frequently.

This is a major weakness because a panel may ask:

  • Were artisans interviewed?
  • Do artisans actually want an online platform?
  • How many artisans currently use Facebook or other marketplaces?
  • Are there measurable sales limitations?

The project needs actual evidence through:

  • surveys
  • interviews
  • community validation
  • sample artisan feedback

2. Scope is Too Large

The proposal currently attempts to include:

  • full e-commerce marketplace
  • artisan management
  • marketing and engagement system
  • administrative platform
  • online transactions

A complete e-commerce system alone is already a large capstone project.

Adding advanced marketing and engagement systems significantly increases complexity.

3. Marketplace Competition Problem

A panel may ask:

"Why not simply use Facebook Marketplace, Shopee, or Lazada?"

The proposal must clearly justify why a dedicated platform is needed.

The strongest justification should focus on:

  • cultural preservation
  • localized artisan identity
  • heritage documentation
  • community-centered promotion

4. System Identity is Unclear

The system currently appears to be both:

  • a marketplace platform
  • a cultural preservation platform

The proponents should clearly define the primary purpose of the system.

Recommended Direction:

A digital platform for showcasing and promoting indigenous arts and crafts with simplified product inquiry and artisan profile management.

Recommended Scope Reduction

Keep

  • User Accounts
  • Artisan Profiles
  • Product Catalog
  • Product Inquiry System
  • Administrative Dashboard
  • Cultural Information Pages

Remove or Simplify

  • Full online payment integration
  • Complex marketing features
  • Advanced engagement systems
  • Large-scale e-commerce logistics
  • Complex order fulfillment workflows

Recommended Problem Statements

  1. Indigenous artisans in Capiz have limited digital platforms for promoting and showcasing their arts and crafts.
  2. Many indigenous products are marketed only within local communities, limiting visibility and potential customers.
  3. There is no centralized digital platform dedicated to preserving and promoting indigenous arts and crafts in Capiz.
  4. Buyers have limited access to information about indigenous artisans and their products.
  5. Indigenous cultural products are underrepresented in digital marketplaces and promotional platforms.

Recommended Objectives

General Objective

To design and develop a web-based platform for promoting and showcasing indigenous arts and crafts in Capiz.

Specific Objectives

  1. To develop a system for managing artisan profiles and product listings.
  2. To provide a digital catalog of indigenous arts and crafts.
  3. To implement a product inquiry and communication feature for interested buyers.
  4. To create an administrative module for managing users and product information.
  5. To promote awareness and visibility of indigenous cultural products through digital technology.

The novelty comes from:

  • localized indigenous artisan promotion
  • cultural preservation integration

The novelty does not come from e-commerce itself because marketplace systems already exist.

Coordinator Verdict

Status: Approved with Major Scope Reduction


Reasons

  • Strong cultural and social relevance.
  • Meaningful community-centered objective.
  • Problem validation is currently weak.
  • Scope is too large for a standard BSIT capstone.
  • The system identity needs clearer definition.

Final Recommendations

  • Validate the problem through artisan interviews and surveys.
  • Reduce the system into a simplified artisan showcase and inquiry platform.
  • Focus on cultural preservation and digital visibility rather than full-scale e-commerce.
  • Avoid complex logistics and payment systems unless already proven feasible.

If the proponents maintain a manageable scope and focus on artisan visibility, cultural preservation, and product showcasing, the project can become a meaningful and realistic BSIT capstone with strong community impact.

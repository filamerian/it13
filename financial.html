Proposed System Analysis

Financial Decision Support System for Fishing Trawl Businesses in Barangay Cantil

Overall Evaluation

This is one of the strongest and most defensible proposals among the capstone projects reviewed. The project addresses a real livelihood-based problem, has clearly identified users, involves meaningful data processing, and aligns well with BSIT competencies.

Unlike many proposals that focus on general record keeping, this project deals with a specific operational process involving fishing trips, expenses, sales, profit computation, and income distribution. The system has a clear purpose and measurable outputs.

Strengths

1. Real and Localized Problem

The proposal is based on an actual business activity in Barangay Cantil.

Current issues include:

  • paper-based recording
  • verbal reporting
  • missing or incomplete records
  • inaccurate profit calculations
  • disputes in income sharing

These are legitimate operational problems.

2. Strong BSIT Alignment

The project involves:

  • database management
  • financial computation
  • reporting
  • dashboards
  • role-based access
  • decision support

These are core BSIT competencies.

3. Clear Stakeholders

Primary users:

  • fishing trawl owners
  • boat captains

The users are specific and easily accessible for testing and validation.

4. Measurable Benefits

The system can directly measure:

  • reduction in computation errors
  • faster report generation
  • improved financial tracking
  • more accurate profit sharing

This makes evaluation easier during defense.

Areas for Improvement

1. Title May Be Overstating the System

Current title:

Financial Decision Support System

A panel may ask:

"What decisions is the system actually supporting?"

Currently the features mainly provide:

  • recording
  • computation
  • reporting

These are more characteristic of a:

Financial Management System

rather than a true Decision Support System (DSS).

A DSS typically includes:

  • trend analysis
  • forecasting
  • recommendations
  • scenario analysis

The current proposal does not yet show these capabilities.

Recommended Titles

Financial Management and Profit Monitoring System for Fishing Trawl Businesses in Barangay Cantil

or

Trip-Based Financial Monitoring System for Fishing Trawl Businesses in Barangay Cantil

These titles are more accurate and easier to defend.

2. Problem Statement Needs More Specificity

Current problem:

Manual and inaccurate financial management.

This is somewhat broad.

A stronger version would identify specific operational issues:

  • delayed recording of expenses
  • incorrect computation of trip profits
  • inconsistent crew income distribution
  • difficulty reviewing previous trips

3. "Smart Recommendations" May Create Problems

The proposal includes:

Smart recommendation tips

This is risky.

A panel may ask:

  • What algorithm is used?
  • What data supports recommendations?
  • Is AI involved?

If the answer is unclear, this feature weakens the proposal.

Recommendation:

  • Remove this feature
  • Or convert it into simple rule-based alerts

Example:

Fuel cost exceeded average trip cost by 20%.

4. Advanced Analytics May Cause Scope Expansion

The system already has:

  • expenses
  • sales
  • profit computation
  • reports
  • dashboards

These are sufficient.

Complex analytics are not necessary for capstone success.

Problem Validation Assessment

The problem is believable but needs stronger evidence.

The group should gather:

  • sample expense records
  • sample sales records
  • owner interviews
  • examples of computation mistakes

This strengthens Chapter 1 significantly.

Recommended Problem Statements

  1. Fishing trawl businesses in Barangay Cantil rely on manual recording of trip expenses and fish sales.
  2. Manual computation of profits and income distribution is prone to errors and inconsistencies.
  3. Business owners and captains have difficulty monitoring the financial performance of individual fishing trips.
  4. Historical financial records are difficult to retrieve and analyze due to paper-based documentation.
  5. There is no centralized system for generating financial reports and monitoring profitability.

Recommended Objectives

General Objective

To design and develop a Financial Management and Profit Monitoring System for Fishing Trawl Businesses in Barangay Cantil.

Specific Objectives

  1. To develop a system for recording fishing trip expenses and fish sales.
  2. To automate the computation of trip profits, losses, and income distribution.
  3. To provide dashboards that summarize financial performance.
  4. To generate trip-based and monthly financial reports.
  5. To maintain a centralized database of financial records for monitoring and analysis.

Scope Recommendations

Keep

  • Expense recording
  • Sales recording
  • Profit computation
  • Crew income computation
  • Dashboard
  • Reports
  • User roles

These are sufficient for a complete capstone.

Remove or Simplify

  • Smart recommendation tips
  • Advanced analytics
  • Complex predictive features

Unless they can clearly justify and implement them.

Progress Assessment

Positive

Already completed:

  • login system
  • role management

This shows actual development has started.

Concern

Core modules remain unfinished:

  • expenses
  • sales
  • computations
  • reporting

These should be prioritized immediately because they form the heart of the system.

Novelty Assessment

Originality: 7/10

Technical Complexity: 7/10

Practical Value: 9/10

Feasibility: 9/10


The novelty does not come from financial management itself, but from its localization to fishing trawl operations and automated crew income computation.

Coordinator Verdict

Status: Approved with Minor Revision


Reasons

  • Real and validated local problem.
  • Strong BSIT alignment.
  • Clear users and measurable outcomes.
  • Manageable scope.
  • Practical deployment potential.

Recommendations

  • Change or justify the term "Decision Support System."
  • Remove vague intelligent features unless clearly defined.
  • Focus on trip-based financial monitoring and profit computation.
  • Gather stronger evidence from actual fishing operators to support the problem statement.

Among the proposals reviewed, this is one of the more realistic, useful, and achievable capstone projects and has a high likelihood of successful completion if the scope remains controlled.

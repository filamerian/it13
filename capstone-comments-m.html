I. Overview of the Current State of Technology
Comments:
This section effectively outlines how technology has transformed sports data handling globally and contrasts it with FCU’s manual recordkeeping practices. However, the shift from global practices to local context could be smoother.
Suggestions:

  • Strengthen the link between international references (e.g., FinalForms) and FCU's operational reality.
  • Add examples from regional collegiate sports programs or CHED-mandated systems.

Recommendations:
Include a comparison table showing data handling differences between manual systems and modern digital platforms, emphasizing benefits like improved accuracy and faster access.

II. Desired State of Technology
Comments:
The section describes a clear vision for a responsive, centralized system with features suited for coaches, trainers, and administrators. Accessibility and usability are emphasized.
Suggestions:

  • Specify if the platform is intended for mobile, web, or both.
  • Clarify communication tools (e.g., report notifications, in-app messaging).

Recommendations:
Introduce a feature summary table that maps modules (e.g., medical records, training schedules) to their intended user interactions and outcomes.

III. Statement of the Problem
Comments:
The current format mixes observations with structural issues. The mention of CHED requirements adds relevance.
Suggestions:
Reformat the discussion into itemized problem statements to increase clarity and academic rigor.
Recommendations:
Use the following structure:

  1. Athlete data is fragmented across documents and Excel sheets, resulting in record duplication and access delays.
  2. Coaches and staff lack a unified platform to retrieve up-to-date athlete profiles during critical decision points.
  3. Injury monitoring and training performance tracking are inconsistently documented, impeding progress evaluation.
  4. FCU lacks a centralized system aligned with CHED’s reporting and compliance needs.
  5. Manual documentation consumes staff time and limits collaboration across departments.

IV. Objectives of the Study
Comments:
The general objective sets a strong direction. The specific objectives are comprehensive but could benefit from measurable phrasing and structure.
Suggestions:
Group objectives by purpose (technical, operational, strategic) and align them with the problem statements.
Recommendations:
General Objective:
To develop a secure, centralized Athlete Management System that digitizes and streamlines the storage, retrieval, and analysis of student-athlete data for Filamer Christian University.
Specific Objectives:

  1. Design modules for managing athlete profiles, training performance, medical records, and academic status.
  2. Implement secure user-role access to protect sensitive information and maintain data integrity.
  3. Create searchable databases that enable efficient data retrieval and update processes.
  4. Provide automated reporting capabilities for performance tracking and regulatory compliance.
  5. Ensure the system is intuitive, accessible, and adaptable to the workflows of coaches, staff, and administrators.

III. Statement of the Problem
Comments:
The problem is clearly articulated with five itemized challenges, offering strong alignment with El Niño mitigation and resource waste.
Suggestions:

  • Strengthen each problem statement by linking it to consequences such as labor inefficiency, plant stress, and economic losses.
  • Consider referencing local climate studies to ground the vulnerability to El Niño.

Recommendations:
Frame each point as follows:

  1. Manual intervention leads to time-intensive labor and inconsistent watering practices.
  2. Fixed watering schedules fail to adapt to actual soil needs, causing water misuse and crop stress.
  3. Traditional systems lack adaptability during El Niño droughts, compromising crop survival.
  4. Absence of remote monitoring prevents timely decision-making and proactive intervention.
  5. Ineffective conservation practices result in unnecessary water loss, straining already scarce resources.

IV. Objectives of the Study
Comments:
The general and specific objectives are logically aligned with the stated problems. They incorporate sensor-driven automation and sustainability.
Suggestions:

  • Reformulate specific objectives using action verbs and measurable outcomes.
  • Categorize into functional, technical, and strategic goals.

Recommendations:
General Objective:
To design and implement an automated IoT-based rainwater harvesting system tailored for agricultural use in Roxas City, enhancing irrigation efficiency and resilience during drought conditions.
Specific Objectives:

  1. Develop a sensor-actuated irrigation system using Esp32 microcontroller for data-driven water distribution.
  2. Integrate real-time soil moisture monitoring to dynamically regulate water flow.
  3. Enable mobile/web remote access with live system feedback, error alerts, and irrigation logs.
  4. Implement drought-resilient protocols to maximize rainwater capture and minimize water loss.
  5. Promote sustainable agricultural practices through resource-efficient irrigation and digital oversight.

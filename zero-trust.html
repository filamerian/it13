Proposed System Analysis

ZERO TRUST: A Mobile-Based Interactive Cybersecurity Game for FCU SHS Students

Overall Evaluation (Capstone Coordinator View)

This proposal has a good educational objective and addresses a real problem, but in its current form it is too broad, lacks a clear system problem, and risks becoming merely an educational game rather than a BSIT capstone project.

The biggest concern is that the group has not clearly identified what specific cybersecurity awareness gap exists among FCU SHS students and how it will be measured.

Strengths

1. Real and Relevant Problem

Cybersecurity threats such as:

  • phishing
  • social engineering
  • fake websites
  • malicious links
  • password attacks

are real and increasing.

This is a valid problem area.

2. Specific Target Users

The project clearly identifies:

FCU Senior High School Students

This is good because:

  • users are identifiable
  • testing is feasible
  • evaluation is manageable

3. Educational Value

The project supports:

  • cybersecurity awareness
  • digital citizenship
  • safe online behavior

This aligns with current educational needs.

Major Weaknesses

1. The Problem Statement is Too Generic

Current problem:

"Phishing is rampant nowadays."

A panel may immediately ask:

"How do you know FCU SHS students are vulnerable to phishing?"
"Do you have data?"

Currently, the proposal assumes the problem exists without proving it.

2. Weak Connection to "Zero Trust"

The title uses:

"ZERO TRUST"

However, Zero Trust is actually a cybersecurity framework involving:

  • verification
  • least privilege access
  • continuous authentication

The proposed features are mostly:

  • educational content
  • storylines
  • simulations

The project does not actually implement Zero Trust concepts beyond awareness.

This creates a title-content mismatch.

3. Risk of Becoming Just a Mobile Game

Panels may ask:

"What makes this a BSIT system and not simply an educational game?"

Currently the proposal lacks:

  • analytics
  • learning assessment mechanisms
  • behavioral tracking
  • measurable cybersecurity learning outcomes

4. No Existing Prototype

Current status:

nothing started

For a game-based capstone, this is concerning because:

  • game development takes longer than expected
  • content creation is time-consuming
  • testing educational effectiveness requires additional time

Problem Validation Needed

Before proceeding, the group should gather evidence.

Possible survey questions:

  • Have you received suspicious links?
  • Can you identify phishing emails?
  • Do you reuse passwords?
  • Have you experienced account compromise?

If results show low awareness, the problem becomes evidence-based.

Current Problem Statement Weakness

Current:

Phishing is rampant nowadays.

This is not enough.

It is a societal problem, not yet an FCU SHS problem.

Recommended Problem Statements

  1. Senior High School students are frequently exposed to online threats such as phishing links, fake websites, and social engineering attacks.
  2. Many students have limited knowledge of how to identify and respond to common cybersecurity threats.
  3. Existing cybersecurity awareness activities are often delivered through lectures or presentations that may not fully engage students.
  4. There is no interactive mobile-based learning tool in FCU that allows students to experience cybersecurity scenarios through simulation and guided decision-making.
  5. There is a need for an engaging digital platform that improves cybersecurity awareness and safe online behavior among students.

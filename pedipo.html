Proposed System Analysis

Web-Based Economic and Investment Information System for PEDIPO Capiz

Overall Evaluation

This proposal addresses a legitimate organizational problem and has a clear target client. PEDIPO's role in investment promotion, economic profiling, and business development makes a centralized information system valuable and relevant.

However, the proposal currently suffers from scope inflation, vague problem definition, and unnecessary AI claims. The core system itself is strong, but the project attempts to add advanced AI features that are not clearly justified and may be difficult to implement and defend.

Strengths

1. Real Organizational Need

The proposal identifies practical operational issues such as:

  • Economic data scattered across files and spreadsheets
  • Slow retrieval of information
  • Difficulty responding to investor inquiries
  • Lack of centralized records

These are legitimate operational problems that can be improved through proper information system implementation.

2. Strong Institutional Client

PEDIPO is a real organization with a clear mandate related to economic development and investment promotion. This provides a defined client, identifiable users, and realistic deployment potential.

3. Good BSIT Alignment

The project involves the following BSIT competencies:

  • Database management
  • Web development
  • Reporting systems
  • Dashboards
  • Information management

4. High Practical Value

The system could help:

  • PEDIPO staff access information faster
  • Investors view opportunities more easily
  • LGUs maintain updated economic profiles
  • Decision-makers generate reports efficiently

Major Concerns

1. Problem Statement is Too Broad

Current problem statement:

"PEDIPO lacked a centralized system."

This statement is too generic. The proposal should identify specific operational issues such as fragmented economic records, delayed report generation, difficulty accessing municipal data, inefficient investor inquiry handling, and lack of centralized investment information.

2. AI Features Are Poorly Defined

The proposal includes:

  • AI-driven economic insights
  • AI-powered data entry

These features may become weaknesses during defense because the proposal does not clearly explain:

  • What AI model will be used
  • What data will train the AI
  • How economic insights will be generated
  • How output accuracy will be validated

Recommendation:

  • Replace AI-driven economic insights with automated trend summaries and data visualization dashboards
  • Replace AI-powered data entry with assisted data encoding or OCR-based document encoding if feasible

3. Real-Time Economic Monitoring is Unrealistic

The proposal claims that the system will automatically track and update economic growth indicators in real time.

This may not be realistic because economic data often depends on:

  • LGU submissions
  • PSA reports
  • Local surveys
  • Manual updates

Recommendation:

Replace “real-time economic monitoring” with “periodic updating and centralized reporting.”

4. Scope Needs Prioritization

The current proposal includes too many modules such as:

  • Dashboards
  • AI features
  • Investor portal
  • Product directory
  • Innovation map
  • Municipal comparison
  • Reporting tools
  • Search features

This creates unnecessary complexity and increases implementation risk.

Recommended Core Scope

Keep

  • Centralized Economic Database
  • Municipal Economic Profiles
  • Investor Information Portal
  • Investment Opportunity Directory
  • Interactive Dashboards
  • Reporting Module
  • Search and Filtering

Optional Features

  • Municipal comparison charts
  • Product directory

Remove or Simplify

  • AI-driven economic insights
  • AI-powered data entry
  • Real-time economic monitoring claims

Recommended Problem Statements

  1. PEDIPO currently relies on fragmented records and spreadsheets for storing economic and investment information.
  2. Staff experience delays in retrieving economic data needed for reports and investor inquiries.
  3. There is no centralized database for maintaining municipal economic profiles and investment information.
  4. Investors have limited access to organized and updated information about investment opportunities in Capiz.
  5. Manual preparation of economic reports is time-consuming and prone to inconsistencies.

Recommended Objectives

General Objective

To design and develop a Web-Based Economic and Investment Information System for PEDIPO Capiz.

Specific Objectives

  1. To develop a centralized database for economic and investment-related information.
  2. To provide a platform for managing municipal economic profiles and investment records.
  3. To develop an investor-facing portal for accessing investment opportunities in Capiz.
  4. To generate reports and dashboards for economic monitoring and analysis.
  5. To provide search and filtering tools for efficient information retrieval.

Coordinator Verdict

Status: Approved with Minor Revision

Recommendations

  • Remove or clearly redefine AI features.
  • Replace “real-time economic monitoring” with realistic periodic data updating.
  • Narrow the scope to information management, reporting, and investment promotion.
  • Strengthen the problem statement using actual PEDIPO workflow issues and user interviews.

This proposal has strong potential because it serves a real government-related organization and solves a genuine information management problem.

If the team avoids unnecessary AI features and focuses on centralized data management and investor information services, the project can become a highly practical and defensible BSIT capstone.

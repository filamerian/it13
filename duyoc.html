Proposed System Analysis

Smart Community Safety Incident System for Brgy. Duyoc, Dao, Capiz

Overall Evaluation

This is a potentially good barangay information system, but the proposal currently has a major weakness:

The group has not yet established that the problem occurs frequently enough to justify building an entire system.

The capstone idea is acceptable, but before approval, the proponents must provide evidence that incident reporting is a recurring operational problem in the barangay.

Major Concern: Frequency and Need Validation

The group answered:

Frequency: Daily

This is the first issue that must be verified.

A panel will likely ask:

  • How many incidents are actually reported per day?
  • What types of incidents occur?
  • Is there documented evidence?
  • How many reports does the barangay process monthly?
  • Is the current process causing significant delays?

At present, the proposal provides no evidence supporting the claim that incidents occur daily.

Risk of Building a System for a Small Problem

Suppose the barangay only records:

  • 2 to 3 incidents per month
  • Occasional disputes
  • Occasional accidents

Then a panel may ask:

Why build a full incident management system for such a low-volume process?

The system becomes difficult to justify.

The proponents must prove that:

  • Incidents occur regularly
  • Records are difficult to manage
  • Report generation consumes significant time
  • Retrieval of past incidents is problematic

Without this evidence, the need for the system becomes weak.

Scope Assessment

Appropriate Features

Keep:

  • Incident Reporting
  • Status Tracking
  • Centralized Database
  • Dashboard
  • Reports
  • Search Function
  • Announcements

These are enough for a complete capstone.

Feature to Reevaluate

GPS-Based Incident Mapping

Ask:

Is precise location tracking actually required by the barangay?

If incidents are simply recorded by sitio or purok, GPS may add complexity without much value.

Feature to Simplify

Real-Time Tanod Alerts

Instead of complex real-time systems, simple SMS alerts, notification logs, or dashboard notifications may already be sufficient.

Recommended Problem Statements

  1. Incident reports in Brgy. Duyoc are currently recorded manually using logbooks.
  2. Manual recording may result in incomplete, inconsistent, or difficult-to-retrieve records.
  3. Barangay officials experience difficulty monitoring the status of reported incidents.
  4. Preparation of incident reports and summaries requires manual consolidation of records.
  5. There is no centralized digital repository for storing and managing incident information.

Recommended Objectives

General Objective

To design and develop a Smart Community Safety Incident System for Brgy. Duyoc, Dao, Capiz.

Specific Objectives

  1. To develop a digital incident reporting system for barangay personnel.
  2. To maintain a centralized database of incident records.
  3. To monitor and update the status of reported incidents.
  4. To generate automated incident reports and summaries.
  5. To provide a dashboard for monitoring community safety incidents.

Conditions

Before Chapter 1 approval, the group must establish:

  • Actual incident frequency

  • Documented problems in the current process
  • Evidence from interviews with barangay officials

Most Important Recommendation

The biggest weakness of this proposal is not the system design.

It is the lack of evidence that the problem is large enough to require a dedicated system.

The proponents should collect actual data such as:

  • Incidents per month
  • Report preparation time
  • Number of missing or incomplete records
  • Feedback from barangay officials

Once the need is quantified, the proposal becomes much stronger and easier to defend during proposal and final defense.

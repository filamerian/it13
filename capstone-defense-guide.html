Thesis/Capstone Prototype Defense Preparation Guide

Chapters 1-3, WORKING Prototype

Section Time Allocation
Introduction (Chapter 1) 3 minutes
Review of Related Literature 2 minutes
Methodology (Chapter 3) 3 minutes
Prototype Demonstration 12 minutes
Q&A from Panel 40 minutes
TOTAL 30 minutes

 

Chapter 1: Introduction (3 mins)

Students should be ready to explain:

  • Project Title & Rationale: Why this system matters, contextualized to the target users.
  • Background of the Study: Real-world problem, institutional relevance, and motivation.
  • Statement of the Problem: Clear major and minor problems, ideally framed as questions.
  • Objectives: General and specific objectives aligned with the problem statement.
  • Scope and Limitations: What the system covers and what it intentionally excludes.
  • Significance of the Study: Who benefits and how - customers, students, administrators, etc.
  • Definition of Terms: Be ready to clarify technical or conceptual terms if asked.

Chapter 2: Review of Related Literature and Studies (2 mins)

Students should prepare to:

  • Summarize Key References: Highlight 3-5 core studies or systems that inspired the project.
  • Explain Relevance: How each reference supports design choices or validates the need.
  • Identify Gaps: What existing systems lack and how their project fills those gaps.
  • Show Ethical Awareness: Cite sources properly and avoid AI-generated bibliographies without verification.

Chapter 3: Methodology (3 mins)

Students should be able to:

  • Describe the Development Model: (e.g., Agile, Waterfall, Spiral), justify its use.
  • Present System Architecture: Diagrams showing frontend/backend, database, APIs, etc.
  • Explain Tools and Technologies: Why they chose Next.js, Tailwind, PostgreSQL, etc.
  • Outline Data Flow and User Roles: How users interact with the system and what data moves where.
  • Discuss Testing Plans: Even if preliminary, unit testing, usability testing, etc.

Sample Prototype (12 mins)

Students must demonstrate:

  • Core Features in Action: Even partial, e.g., login, counseling request, peer forum.
  • UI/UX Design: Show mobile responsiveness, accessibility, and user-friendly flows.
  • Security Measures: Highlight login protection, data privacy (RA 10173 compliance).
  • Anonymity & Support Features: Showcase how the system reduces stigma and encourages help-seeking.
  • Feedback Mechanisms: If included, ratings, suggestions, or counselor notes.

Q&A from Panel (40 mins)

  • Prepare for technical, ethical, and contextual questions
  • Anticipate Questions: On feasibility, originality, ethical use of AI, and system scalability.

Presentation & Defense Tips

  • Prepare 10-15 slides: Covering Chapters 1-3 and the prototype walkthrough.
  • Assign Roles: Each member should present a section and answer related questions.
  • Practice Demo Flow: Ensure the prototype runs smoothly or have screenshots as backup.
  • Bring Documentation: Provide 2 hard copies for the panel. Submit final softcopy of Chapters 1-3 on or before Tuesday, 30 September 2025

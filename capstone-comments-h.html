ONLINE COUNSELING AND SUPPORT SYSTEM FOR FCU HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS

Recommendations

Chapter I – Introduction

Strengths:

  • Clearly identifies the problem: lack of accessible counseling services for high school students.

  • Justifies importance (mental health, accessibility, support).

  • States objectives (developing an online counseling and support system).

Recommendations:

  1. Strengthen Problem Statement by including:

    • Local statistics on student stress/mental health (from DOH, DepEd, WHO).

    • Specific counseling challenges in your school (e.g., limited number of counselors, confidentiality issues).

  2. Revise Significance of the Study:

    • Expand beyond students—include parents, teachers, school administrators, and future researchers.

  3. Clarify Scope and Delimitation:

    • Define whether the system will provide real-time chat, appointment booking, or mental health resources.

    • State limitations: system is not a replacement for professional psychiatric help.

Chapter II – Review of Related Literature

Strengths:

  • Good mix of local and foreign studies.

  • Mentions online counseling systems, digital mental health apps, and accessibility issues.

Recommendations:

  1. Reorganize RRL:

    • Start with global studies (WHO, UNICEF, APA studies).

    • Narrow down to Philippine context (DepEd initiatives, CHED mental health policies).

    • End with similar IT systems (case studies on online counseling platforms).

  2. Add a Synthesis Matrix/Table:

    • Columns: Author/Year | System/Study | Features/Findings | Research Gap.

    • This makes it clear how your study fills a gap.

  3. Highlight the gap: Most systems focus on college/university level, but few target high school students in provincial/private schools.

Chapter III – Methodology

Strengths:

  • Identifies the system to be developed.

  • Mentions phases of development.

Recommendations:

  1. System Development Methodology:

    • Use Agile (Scrum) or Modified Waterfall, as both are CHED-approved approaches for capstone.

    • Show development phases: Planning → Design → Development → Testing → Deployment → Maintenance.

  2. System Architecture:

    • Provide diagrams: Context Diagram, Data Flow Diagram (DFD), Use Case Diagram.

    • Include a proposed ERD (Entity Relationship Diagram) for database design.

  3. Tools & Technologies:

    • List programming languages (e.g., PHP/Laravel, Python/Django, Node.js).

    • Indicate database (MySQL, Firebase).

    • Frameworks for front-end (React, Angular, Vue).

    • Hosting platform (cloud vs. on-prem).

  4. Testing & Evaluation:

    • Define evaluation criteria:

      • Functionality (does it work as intended?).

      • Usability (is it easy for students and counselors?).

      • Reliability (system uptime).

      • Security (confidentiality of student records).

    • Recommend using ISO 25010 Software Quality Model for evaluation.

  5. Ethical Considerations:

    • Add a section on confidentiality, privacy, and consent (Data Privacy Act of 2012, RA 10173).

    • Explain that student data will be encrypted and only accessible to authorized counselors.

Other Areas to Improve

  • Title refinement: “Online Counseling and Support System for FCU High School Students” could be rephrased for impact:
    “A Web-Based Counseling and Support System for FCU High School Students”

  • Objectives: Make them SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). Example:

    • To design a secure online counseling system accessible to at least 80% of FCU high school students within one semester.

  • References: Ensure APA 7th edition consistency (italicize journal names, add DOIs/URLs).

Overall Verdict

The proposal is acceptable but needs refinement:

  • Strengthen the problem context and significance.

  • Add technical depth (architecture, methodology, evaluation).

  • Address ethical and privacy issues.

  • Improve the literature review with synthesis.

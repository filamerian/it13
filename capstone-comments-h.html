III. Statement of the Problem
Comments:
Captures emotional and psychological barriers such as fear of judgment, shyness, lack of peer connection, and absence of anonymity.
Suggestions:
Convert into formal, itemized statements to align with academic conventions. Reference psychological concepts such as social avoidance, stigma, and self-concealment to strengthen the rationale.
Recommendations:
Structure the problems as:

  1. Students experience hesitance in seeking counseling due to shyness and fear of stigma.
  2. Lack of anonymity discourages open communication about sensitive issues.
  3. Absence of shared spaces limits peer support and social validation.
  4. Fear of being judged prevents students from accessing mental health services.
  5. Counseling referrals and recordkeeping lack centralized coordination, leading to delays and missed follow-ups.

IV. Objectives of the Study
Comments:
The general objective and specific goals align with the problem set and emphasize communication, confidentiality, accessibility, and monitoring.
Suggestions:
Refine wording for clarity and group objectives thematically (Technical, Operational, Strategic). Incorporate measurable outcomes (e.g., usage rates, user satisfaction, appointment attendance).
Recommendations:
General Objective:
To design and develop a secure, user-centered online counseling platform for FCU High School that facilitates real-time support, peer interaction, and efficient session management while ensuring privacy and accessibility.
Specific Objectives:

  1. Implement synchronous and asynchronous communication tools including chat, video calls, and feedback features.
  2. Provide anonymous group discussion spaces categorized by mental health, academic stress, or marginalized identities.
  3. Enforce data privacy and security protocols compliant with Philippine laws (e.g., Data Privacy Act of 2012).
  4. Create a streamlined referral system linking advisers to counselors with tracking capabilities.
  5. Build session history logs and analytic tools to monitor student engagement and outcomes.

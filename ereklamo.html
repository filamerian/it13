Proposed System Analysis

e-Reklamo: A Community Complaint System for Roxas City Traffic Management Unit (TMU)

Overall Evaluation

This is a strong and highly relevant civic-oriented BSIT capstone proposal. Compared to many student projects, this system has a clear real-world use case, identifiable stakeholders, and direct public service value.

The proposal addresses a legitimate operational problem involving traffic complaint reporting, complaint monitoring, and centralized record management within the Roxas City Traffic Management Unit (TMU).

However, while the core concept is strong, the proposal currently suffers from overloaded features, unclear scope boundaries, and risky data privacy concerns related to AI functionality and vehicle information tracking.

With proper refinement and scope control, this can become a highly defensible and realistic BSIT capstone project.

Strengths

1. Strong Real-World Problem

The proposal identifies a legitimate government service inefficiency:

  • citizens must physically go to TMU offices to file complaints
  • reporting processes are slow and inconvenient
  • complaint records are not centralized
  • follow-up monitoring is inefficient

These are strong and valid information system problems.

2. Clear Stakeholders

The users are well-defined:

  • citizens / complainants
  • TMU officers and administrators

This supports:

  • realistic testing
  • practical deployment
  • measurable evaluation

3. Strong BSIT Alignment

The proposal includes:

  • web and mobile system development
  • database management
  • role-based access control
  • notification systems
  • report management
  • evidence uploading

These are core BSIT competencies.

4. High Practical Value

If implemented properly, the system can:

  • improve complaint reporting efficiency
  • centralize complaint records
  • improve complaint monitoring
  • reduce manual workload for TMU personnel

Major Areas for Improvement

1. AI Chatbot is Unnecessary

The proposal includes:

AI support chatbot

This feature introduces unnecessary complexity and may become difficult to justify during defense.

Possible panel questions include:

  • What AI model will be used?
  • How will responses be generated?
  • How accurate is the chatbot?
  • What training data is available?

Recommendation:

  • Replace AI chatbot with a simple FAQ system
  • Use guided complaint submission forms
  • Focus on complaint processing rather than conversational AI

2. Plate Number to Personal Information is Risky

The proposal suggests:

Using plate numbers to track personal information of violators

This creates serious concerns regarding:

  • Data Privacy Act compliance
  • unauthorized access to personal data
  • ethical handling of sensitive information
  • possible misuse of identity information

Unless the system has official government integration authorization, this feature may become a major issue during evaluation.

Recommendation:

  • Store plate numbers only as reference records
  • Avoid extracting personal identity information
  • Limit the system to complaint and violation tracking only

3. Scope Boundary Between Complaint System and Law Enforcement System

The proposal risks becoming:

a surveillance or law enforcement platform

instead of:

a complaint intake and tracking system

This distinction is important for ethics, privacy, and capstone approval.

The project should focus on:

  • complaint submission
  • complaint processing
  • complaint monitoring
  • resolution tracking

4. Feature Overload

Current features include:

  • complaint submission
  • evidence uploading
  • violation tracking
  • repeat offender tracking
  • AI chatbot
  • notifications
  • dashboards
  • analytics

This creates scope creep and increases implementation risk.

Recommended Scope Refinement

Keep

  • User registration and login
  • Complaint submission
  • Complaint categorization
  • Image and video evidence upload
  • Complaint tracking status
  • Officer dashboard
  • Notification system

Modify

  • Repeat violator tracking ? limit to complaint history only
  • Vehicle lookup ? internal record reference only

Remove or Simplify

  • AI chatbot
  • Personal identity extraction
  • Overly advanced analytics
  • Complex surveillance-like monitoring

Recommended Problem Statements

  1. Citizens in Roxas City experience difficulty reporting traffic violations due to the need for physical submission at the TMU office.
  2. TMU officers currently rely on manual or fragmented methods for recording and tracking complaints.
  3. There is no centralized digital platform for managing traffic-related complaints and supporting evidence.
  4. Complaint follow-up and monitoring processes are inefficient for both citizens and TMU personnel.
  5. Traffic violation evidence is not systematically organized and stored in a centralized database.

Recommended Objectives

General Objective

To design and develop a web and mobile-based complaint management system that streamlines traffic violation reporting and tracking for Roxas City TMU.

Specific Objectives

  1. To develop a mobile-based complaint submission system for citizens.
  2. To implement a web-based dashboard for TMU officers to manage complaints.
  3. To provide secure upload and storage of complaint evidence.
  4. To implement complaint categorization and tracking from submission to resolution.
  5. To generate complaint history and monitoring reports for TMU personnel.

Key Insight for Defense

The strongest aspect of this project is not AI, surveillance, or criminal tracking.

The true strength of the system is the digitalization and centralization of traffic complaint reporting and monitoring workflows.

That alone is already sufficient for a strong and realistic BSIT capstone.

Coordinator Verdict

Status: Approved with Minor Refinement


Conditions for Approval

  • Remove or simplify the AI chatbot.
  • Avoid personal identity extraction using plate numbers.
  • Focus on complaint management rather than surveillance.
  • Ensure compliance with privacy and ethical standards.
  • Maintain a manageable and realistic scope.

Final Coordinator Comment

This is a strong, practical, and highly relevant BSIT capstone proposal because it addresses a real government service inefficiency with clear operational value.

The project has a high probability of successful implementation if the group maintains strict scope control and focuses on:

complaint submission ? complaint processing ? complaint tracking ? resolution monitoring

If properly refined, this can become one of the stronger and more deployable capstone systems in the batch.

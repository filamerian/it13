Proposed System Analysis

e-Reklamo: A Community Complaint System for Roxas City Traffic Management Unit (TMU)

Overall Evaluation

This is a strong, relevant, and highly defendable BSIT capstone project focused on digitalizing a real public service workflow.

The core concept is solid, but it needs tight scope control and proper justification of advanced features, especially the chatbot.

With refinement, this becomes a very good capstone with strong civic impact and practical deployment value.

Key Strengths

  • Real civic problem (manual complaint submission)
  • Clear users (citizens and TMU officers)
  • Strong BSIT alignment (web, mobile, and database system)
  • High practical value for local government efficiency
  • Easy to evaluate using real users

Refined System Justification

Citizens lack a fast, centralized, and accessible way to report traffic violations, while TMU officers lack an organized system to manage, track, and respond to complaints efficiently.

This leads to:

  • delayed reporting
  • lost or untracked complaints
  • inefficient manual record-keeping
  • poor feedback loop between citizens and TMU

Refined Core System Concept

The system is NOT a surveillance tool.

A digital complaint intake and tracking system that connects citizens and TMU officers through a centralized platform.

Refined Core Features

Citizen (Mobile App)

  • Submit complaint
  • Upload photo/video evidence
  • Select violation type
  • Track complaint status
  • View complaint history

TMU Officer (Web System)

  • Receive and manage complaints
  • Assign complaint status
  • View complaint dashboard
  • Filter by violation type or location
  • Generate reports

Justification: Why a Chatbot is Included

Proposed Feature

AI-assisted chatbot or guided complaint assistant

Justification for Inclusion

1. Helps Citizens Who Do Not Know Proper Complaint Procedures

Many users:

  • do not know what category their complaint belongs to
  • are unsure what evidence is required
  • are unfamiliar with formal reporting language

The chatbot acts as a guided intake assistant that helps users properly file complaints.

2. Reduces Incorrect or Incomplete Submissions

Without guidance:

  • users may submit unclear reports
  • missing evidence may delay processing
  • wrong categories reduce efficiency

With chatbot:

  • the system can prompt required details step-by-step

3. Improves System Usability for Non-Technical Users

Since the target users are general citizens, not technical users, the chatbot functions as a guided interface that reduces confusion and improves complaint completion rates.

4. Can Be Implemented Without Full AI Complexity

The chatbot does NOT need advanced AI implementation.

It can simply be:

  • rule-based chatbot
  • decision-tree guided assistant
  • scripted FAQ assistant

Example prompts:

  • “What type of violation are you reporting?”
  • “Do you have photo evidence?”

5. Supports Government Service Accessibility

The chatbot supports public accessibility, especially for:

  • elderly users
  • low digital literacy users

Important Refinement (Panel-Safe Version)

“A rule-based guided complaint assistant that helps users properly categorize and complete traffic violation reports.”

It should NOT be presented as:

  • AI surveillance
  • intelligent policing system
  • predictive analysis tool

Scope Control Recommendations

Keep

  • complaint submission system
  • status tracking
  • officer dashboard
  • evidence upload
  • notifications
  • rule-based chatbot

Avoid

  • AI crime prediction
  • personal identity extraction from plate numbers
  • surveillance or tracking automation beyond complaints

Revised Problem Statements

  1. Citizens in Roxas City lack a centralized and accessible platform for reporting traffic violations.
  2. TMU officers rely on manual or fragmented systems for recording and tracking complaints.
  3. Complaint submissions are often incomplete due to lack of guidance during reporting.
  4. There is no unified system for monitoring complaint status and resolution.
  5. Communication between citizens and TMU regarding complaints is slow and inefficient.

Revised Objectives

General Objective

To design and develop a mobile and web-based traffic complaint management system that streamlines reporting, tracking, and resolution of traffic violations in Roxas City.

Specific Objectives

  1. To develop a mobile application for submitting traffic violation complaints.
  2. To implement a web-based dashboard for TMU officers to manage complaints.
  3. To allow secure upload and storage of complaint evidence.
  4. To provide real-time complaint status tracking for citizens.
  5. To develop a rule-based chatbot that guides users in properly filing complaints.

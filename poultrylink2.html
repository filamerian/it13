Statement of the Problem

Poultry trading in Capiz is still commonly done through manual and paper-based processes for transactions, product listing, and compliance documentation. Because of this setup, operations are often slow, less organized, and lack transparency, especially in terms of product origin and vendor verification. At present, there is no centralized web-based system that integrates multi-vendor poultry trading with product traceability, compliance validation, and organized monitoring tools for administrators. These challenges make it difficult for sellers, buyers, and administrators to manage poultry trading efficiently and reliably.

Specifically, this study seeks to address the following problems:

  1. Poultry trading in Capiz is still done using manual and paper-based processes, resulting in slow, unorganized, and error-prone transactions.
  2. There is no centralized web-based platform that allows multiple poultry sellers to manage their products, storefronts, and orders in one system.
  3. Buyers have limited access to clear and reliable information regarding the origin and traceability of poultry products.
  4. Compliance documents such as government-issued permits and inspection certificates are still stored in physical form, making verification slow, manual, and dependent on physical checking.
  5. There is no centralized system that allows administrators to properly monitor transactions, vendor activities, and system performance through organized reports.

Objectives of the Study

General Objective

The general objective of this study is to develop a web-based poultry trading and traceability system that addresses issues in manual transaction processing, lack of centralized marketplace management, limited product transparency, inefficient compliance verification, and insufficient administrative monitoring in Capiz.

Specific Objectives

The specific objectives of the study are aligned with the identified problems as follows:

  1. To design and develop a web-based system that addresses manual and paper-based poultry trading processes by automating transactions, product listings, and order management to improve efficiency and organization.
  2. To design and construct a multi-vendor marketplace system that enables poultry sellers to manage their storefronts, products, and customer orders within a centralized platform.
  3. To develop and integrate a product traceability feature that provides buyers with clear and reliable information regarding the origin and source of poultry products.
  4. To design and implement a compliance management module that allows users to upload government-issued permits and inspection certificates for structured and systematic validation.
  5. To develop and deploy an administrative dashboard and reporting system that enables monitoring of transactions, vendor activities, and overall system performance.

What You Should Build First (50% Scope)

Based on your PoultryLink system, your first 50% should focus on core operations:

1. User Authentication System

  • Login and registration
  • Role-based access (admin, seller, buyer)

2. Seller Module (Core Function)

  • Create and manage storefront
  • Add, edit, delete products

3. Buyer Module (Basic Flow)

  • Browse products
  • View product details
  • Place orders (basic transaction flow)

4. Admin Module (Minimum Features)

  • View users
  • Approve or manage sellers
  • View basic transactions

5. Database Setup

  • Users table
  • Products table
  • Orders table

What Belongs to the Second 50%

These are advanced or enhancement features:

  • Product traceability system
  • Compliance document upload and verification
  • Reports and analytics dashboard
  • Advanced monitoring tools
  • Audit logs and notifications

Recommended 50% Completion Standard

Your system is considered 50% working if:

  • Users can log in and register
  • Sellers can manage products
  • Buyers can place orders
  • Admin can view and control basic system data
  • Database is fully connected and working

Important Development Advice

Start development in this order:

Summary

The ChurchHub project addresses the need for a centralized system to manage church operations such as membership, events, announcements, and financial records. The proposal is well-written and organized. It demonstrates a clear understanding of local needs and offers a practical solution. Strengths include strong context, use of Task-Technology Fit and IPO frameworks, and comprehensive related literature. Weaknesses include objectives written as modules, limited technical details on security and financial transparency, and lack of database schema or evaluation instrument details.

Detailed Evaluation by Section

Chapter I - Introduction

ItemEvaluation
Problem Definition

The problem is clearly identified: fragmented manual processes in managing membership, events, finances, and communication.

Revision Needed: Restate as one General Problem and 3–5 Specific Problems for clarity.

General Problem

The Lord of the Nations Church does not have a centralized system to efficiently manage membership records, events, communications, and financial information, resulting in inefficiencies and challenges in administration.

Specific Problems

  1. Membership records are maintained manually, leading to inconsistencies, errors, and difficulty in updating information.

  2. Event planning and attendance tracking are done through paper forms or verbal updates, causing delays and poor coordination.

  3. Church announcements and prayer requests are disseminated informally, which often results in members missing important information.

  4. Financial records lack transparency and accuracy, making it hard to generate timely reports for accountability.

  5. Historical data on members and activities is not systematically archived, reducing the ability to track growth and participation trends.
Objectives

Objectives are aligned but written as module descriptions.

Revision Needed: Emphasize outcomes instead of features.
Example:

Main Objective

To develop a centralized Church Management System for The Lord of the Nations Church that streamlines membership records, event management, communications, and financial tracking, thereby improving administrative efficiency, transparency, and member engagement.

Specific Objectives

To achieve the main objective, the study aims to:

  1. To digitize and organize membership records to ensure accuracy, consistency, and ease of updates.

  2. To implement an event management module that tracks planning, scheduling, and attendance efficiently.

  3. To create a communication platform for announcements and prayer requests to ensure timely dissemination to all members.

  4. To develop a financial management system that improves transparency, accuracy, and reporting of church funds.

  5. To archive historical data on members and church activities for monitoring growth, participation trends, and decision-making.
Theoretical & Conceptual Framework

Task-Technology Fit (TTF) and IPO are appropriate and well-explained.

Suggestion: Add another theory (e.g., DeLone & McLean IS Success Model) to strengthen evaluation criteria.
Scope & Limitations

Scope is clear, covering membership, events, finances, and announcements. Limitations are partly addressed.

Revision Needed: Clarify exclusions (e.g., no payroll, no advanced accounting) and state privacy/data retention policy.
Significance

Well-written with clear benefits for church staff, leaders, and members.

Suggestion: Add measurable impacts such as “reduce time to prepare reports by 50%.”

Chapter III - Methodology

ItemEvaluation
Development Method

Agile is appropriate. Iterative phases (plan, design, develop, test, deploy, review, launch) are described.

Revision Needed: In your Agile plan, specify how long each sprint will last (for example, two weeks or one month) and list the outputs you expect to finish in every sprint, such as prototype of the membership module, event scheduling feature, or testing reports. This will make your timeline and progress easier to follow.
Design & Technical Plan

Modules are described.

Revision Needed: Add ERD/database schema, authentication flow, and backup strategy.
Testing & Evaluation

Functional, usability, and security testing are mentioned.

Revision Needed: Connect your evaluation to a recognized standard, such as ISO 25010 or common usability guidelines. Then, set clear targets for success, for example, aiming for an average rating of 4 out of 5 in the user satisfaction survey.

Prioritized Revision Checklist

  1. Follow the indentions and format in the template provided.
  2. Rewrite Statement of the Problem into one General and numbered Specific Problems.
  3. Revise objectives to focus on outcomes, not just module creation.
  4. Add database schema, authentication design, and backup plan.
  5. Provide sprint length and backlog samples in Agile plan.
  6. Strengthen synthesis in RRL by linking gaps to ChurchHub features.
  7. Define evaluation metrics using ISO 25010 or usability models with acceptance targets.
  8. Clarify scope exclusions and include basic data privacy and retention policy.
